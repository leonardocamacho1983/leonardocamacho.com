# Editorial Standards

## Quality Bar
- Every draft must have a thesis, argument structure, and conclusion.
- Claims must be falsifiable or at least operationally testable.
- Avoid summary-only writing; include interpretation and decision value.

## Structure
- Title
- Lead (problem + claim)
- 3 to 5 sections with explicit sub-arguments
- Final section: implications and next steps

## Evidence and Specificity
- Use concrete language (teams, systems, processes, metrics, incentives).
- Prefer examples and mechanisms over abstractions.
- Name uncertainty when evidence is incomplete.

## Clarity Rules
- One idea per paragraph.
- Limit sentence length variance but avoid mechanical rhythm.
- Remove redundant qualifiers and repeated claims.
