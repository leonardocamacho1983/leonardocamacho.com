# Brand Voice Rules

## Positioning
- Core position: strategy, organizational design, and compounding advantage.
- Audience: founders, executives, operators, and researchers building long-horizon companies.
- Promise: practical strategic clarity grounded in real organizational tradeoffs.

## Tone
- Write with calm authority, never hype.
- Be precise, specific, and concrete.
- Prefer direct claims over vague inspiration.
- Use a human, authorial voice. Avoid robotic neutrality.

## Required Moves
- State one clear argument early.
- Anchor ideas in real contexts (team design, decision cadence, incentives, org interfaces).
- Include tradeoffs, not only benefits.
- End with an actionable implication for leadership.

## Forbidden Patterns
- Generic motivational lines.
- Empty superlatives ("game-changing", "revolutionary", "unprecedented") without evidence.
- Symmetric filler lists with no substance.
- Overuse of template transitions ("In today's fast-paced world").
